Brief History of Workers struggles in Germany

  • Guilds during the Medieval period
  • Formations of first unions with the 1848 revolution
  • Anti‚ÄëSocialist Laws (1878 - 1890) by Otto von Bismarck
  • General Commission of German Trade Unions in 1890
  • General German Trade Union Federation (ADGB) in 1920 during the Weimar Republic.
  • Abolisiton of Unions by the Nazis in 1933
  • German Trade Union Confederation (Deutscher Gewerkschaftsbund, DGB) in 1949

Brief History of Migrant Workers struggles in Germany

  • Guest Workers (Gastarbeiter) in 1950s
  • Ausl√§ndergesetz in 1965
  • Unions weren't interested in migrant workers and hostile to them
  • Wildcat strikes in 1973 at Pierburg and Ford by Turkish workers
    • IG Metall and DGB were against the strikes
    • BILD smear campaign against the workers
    • At Ford, "Counter-Demonstration" by armed thugs, superviosrs, and eventually the police were used to break the strike violently
    • At Pierburg, the wildcat strike was successful
  • Wildcat strikes are illegal in Germany.
    • Only unions can approve strikes.
  • Aufenthaltsgesetz (Residence Act) in 2005

What to check in the contract?

  • Always check the German version of the contract.
  • Check Salary, payment date, place of work, vacations, working time.

  • Stock options and its maturity.
  • Everything must be writing. Promises are just fantasies!

Your rights while working

  • Work time is defined in Arbeitszeitgesetz (ArbZG)
  • Minimum 4 weeks of vacation.
  • Working time is average 8 hours per day of max 6 days a week .
  • Rest period of at least: 30min (6-9 hrs of work) & 45min for more.
  • Working On-call is considered working time. On-call standby is not.
  • You have the right of uninterrupted 11 hours of rest (¬ß 5 ArbZG)
  • Documentation of overtime is required (¬ß 16 ArbZG). (8hrs/day rule)

Part-time

  • Part-Time and Temporary Work is defined in (Teilzeit- und Befristungsgesetz, TzBfG)
  • You are entitled to part time (¬ß 8 TzBfG) if:
    • Employed for more than 6 months
    • Company has more than 15 employees
    • You haven't applied for part-time in the last 2 years
  • At least 3 months before the desired date.
  • Employer must reply in 1 month or request is considered approved.
  • Employer has the right to refuse for operational reasons.
  • You don't have to provide a reason for the switch.
  • No legal entitlement to return back to full-time.

Part-time

  • Temporary Part-time (Br√ºckenteilzeit) is possible (¬ß 9a TzBfG):
    • Starting from 1 year and up to 5 years.
    • Return to full-time is possible.
    • You must be a full-time employee for 6 months.
    • Company has more than 45 employees.
    • If employer has less than 200 employees, part-time approval can be also affected by number of part-time employees.

Education Leave

  • It's defined in the Bildungsurlaubsgesetz and differs by state.
  • In Berlin, it is defined in the Berliner Bildungszeitgesetz (BiZeitG)
  • You have the right to take up to 5 days of paid leave for education.
  • In Berlin, you can take up to 10 days together of paid leave for education per two years.
  • You can take the leave for language courses, political education and other courses.
  • List of courses and places can be found here: Bildungsurlaub
  • Only Bavaria and Saxony don't have Bildungsurlaub laws.

Dismisal

  • Before Probation:
    • Notice period of 2 weeks.
    • No reason is required to terminate. Work council can't object.
    • Fire can even happen during pregenancy or holidays
  • After Probation:
    • Notice period of at least 4 weeks.
    • Notice period increases by staying longer in the company (¬ß 622 BGB).
    • Employers need to consult the work council.
    • There is only 3 reasons for termination: Personal, Behavioral, Operational.

Dismisal

  • Dismissal protection is defined in Dismissal Protection Act (KSchG)
  • There is only 3 reasons for termination:
    • Personal: (e.g., incapacity, poor performance, or misconduct).
    • Behavioral: (e.g., violations of workplace rules or policies).
    • Operational: (e.g., restructuring, downsizing, or economic necessity).
  • ¬ß 1a KSchG defines serverance pay on the conditions:
    1. You didn't file a lawsuit against the termination.
    2. You were laid of for operational reasons.
    3. Employer have mentioned you will be entitled to it.
  • Dismisal is only valid if it's in writing, signed by the employer and you have received it. Email and messages are not valid. (¬ß 623 BGB)

Dismisal

  • Employer will try to make you sign a mutal agreement document "Aufhebungsvertrag". You have the right to refuse!
  • The document will waive your rights to file a lawsuit.
  • Agentur f√ºr Arbeit might deduct 3 months of unemployment benefits if you sign it.
  • Decision to sign it will depend on your situation and the market demand.

Dismisal

  • Legal insurance will cover the costs of the lawsuit (Highly recommended!).
  • If you don't have a legal insurance, fees are calculated based on a table of fees (RVG).
  • If you can't afford the fees, you can apply for Prozesskostenhilfe (PKH).
  • Each party pays their own lawyer fees.
    • Exception: in case of appeal, the loser pays all the fees.
  • The legal process is to make sure the termination was legal and a fair social selection was made.

Dismisal

  • Legal sequence will be:
    1. The lawyer will discuss with you the strength of your case.
    2. A lawsuit will be filed.
    3. There will be an initial court hearing to reach a resolution.
    4. If no resolution, there will be a court trial with a judge.
    5. There will be a final verdict.
    6. Appeal is possible!
  • Filing a lawsuit as a group can put more pressure on the employer if it's coordinated.

Resigning

  • You might be still required to work during the notice period.
  • Your unemployement benefits will be reduced by 3 months.
  • If you resign for medical reasons, you might not lose the 3 months.
  • It's better to apply for medical leave instead of resigning.
  • It's covered by your employer usually for the first 6 weeks of illness and then your health insurance will cover it for up to 78 weeks.
  • Mental health issues and burnouts are also considered as medical reasons.
  • ‚ÄºÔ∏è Your employer has no right to know the reason for your medical condition.
  • ‚ÄºÔ∏è Start the process by only consulting your Hausarzt (General Doctor).

Unemployment

  • It's defined in the Social Code Book III (SGB III)
  • Your point of contact is the Agentur f√ºr Arbeit (not the Job Center).
  • Applying can be done online or in person.
  • You are entitled to unemployment benefits if you have worked for more than 12 months in the last 30 months.
  • You will get 60% of your last salary if you have no children. 67% if you have children.
  • You will get paid half the number of months you have worked up to 12 months. It increases by old age.
  • There is a maximum of amount paid out and it's calculated using this calculator.

Unemployment

  • You can still work while being unemployed
  • You can only earn a maximum of 165‚Ç¨ per month
  • Earning more will reduce your benefits
  • Otherwise, you can pause your benefits and resume later without affecting the total amount paid out.

Start-up grant (Gr√ºndungszuschuss)

  • Your can switch from being unemployed to self-employed.
  • You will get your unemployement benefits + 300‚Ç¨ for up to 6 months. Then Phase 2 with 300‚Ç¨ for 9 months.
  • This doesn't affect your Income Tax.
  • You will have to pay your own health insurance and pension fees!
  • You will need to prepare a business and financial plan to apply.
  • There is no legal entitlement to get the grant.

Workers Council

  • To reduce all this burden, you can start a work council instead!
  • They are like the mini-union in your workplace.
  • Special Dimissal Protection (¬ß 15 KSchG)
    • If you start a work council, you can't be fired for at least 6 months.
    • If you were elected to the work council, you can't be fired for 1 year.
  • You can start a work council if you have more than 5 employees eligible to vote.
  • Berlin Tech Workers Coalition is a great place to start and get support for creating a work council in tech sector.
  • Being a union member helps to get more support from the unions (ex: Ver.di or IG Metall).
  • Collective Solidarity instead of Self-Survivalism ü´Ç

Workers Council

Work Council has many benefits:
  • Ensure everyone is treated fairly
  • Can be in the supervisory board of the company
  • Check out companies finances and balance sheets
  • Approve or reject company policies
  • Approve or reject company layoffs
  • Negotiate with the employer for better and safer conditions

Workers Council

  • Work councils can NOT get involved in baraging for collective agreements (ex: Increasing salaries, Holidays and working hours)
  • For this, the union needs to be involved.
  • Participating in a union gives migrants political power.

Discrimination and racism

  • Discrimination is defined in the General Equal Treatment Act (AGG)
  • Discrimination based on race, ethnic origin, gender, religion, disability, age, or sexual orientation is prohibited.
  • You can file a lawsuit against the employer if you have been discriminated against.
  • Work councils oversight can help to prevent discrimination.
  • HR is not your friend. They are there to protect the company.
  • Check out Germany's Discrimination handbook for more details
  • To fight or report German police racism, reach out to KOP Berlin.
